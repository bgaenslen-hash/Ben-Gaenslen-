Bucks Hackathon Prompt 2

Milwaukee Bucks Fan Engagement & Customer Journey Analysis

Objective

Analyze fan behaviors to optimize engagement, ticketing strategies, and long-term retention by understanding the customer lifetime journey.

1. Map the Customer Journey Stages

Define customer segments based on historical purchasing behavior:

  • Casual Fan: Buys single-game tickets occasionally.
  • Engaged Fan: Purchases multiple single-game tickets or group tickets.
  • Partial Plan Holder: Purchases partial plan packages.
  • Season Ticket Prospect: Has engaged at a high level but hasnt committed to full season tickets.
  • Season Ticket Member (STM): Highest level of commitment.

Tasks:

  • Segment customers based on historical behaviors.
  • Identify transition probabilities between these stages over time.

2. Analyze Key Drivers of Engagement & Retention

Use historical ticketing, spending, and engagement data to uncover:

  • What behaviors lead to fans moving to a higher tier?
    • (e.g., What % of Casual Fans convert to Partial Plan Holders?)
  • What indicators show churn risk?
    • (e.g., Inactivity, drop in spending, low digital engagement)

3. Build Predictive Models

  • Propensity Model: Predict likelihood of fans progressing to the next engagement tier.
  • Churn Model: Predict risk of disengagement or cancellation.

4. Provide Business Insights & Recommendations

  • Suggest data-driven marketing and retention strategies:
    • Targeted offers
    • Email campaigns
    • Upsell promotions
  • Propose engagement initiatives to increase Customer Lifetime Value (CLV):
    • Loyalty programs
    • Exclusive content
    • Personalized experiences

def assign_season_key(date):
    # make sure it's a datetime
    date = pd.to_datetime(date)
    year = date.year
    month = date.month
    
    # Example: NBA season starts in Oct
    if month >= 10:  
        return f"{year}-{year+1}"
    else:  
        return f"{year-1}-{year}"

def assign_stage(row):
    products = row["product_types"]

    if products == 0:
        return "Casual Fan"
    elif 'Renewal Full' in products or 'New Full' in products:
        return "STM"
    elif "Partial Plan" in products  or 'Half Plans' in products or 'Flex Plans' in products:
        # could analyze more advanced stats about what are current STMs spending on retail and other stuff that would help indicate how perspective members are similar
        if row["retail_spend"] > 100 and row["n_purchases"] > 25: 
            return "Season Ticket Prospect"
        else: 
            return "Partial Plan Holder"
    elif row["n_purchases"] > 3:  # multiple single-game/group purchases
        return "Engaged Fan"
    elif row["n_purchases"] > 0:
        return "Casual Fan"
    #elif row["n_purchases"]<=1 and row["retail_spend"]==0:
        #return "Lapsed Fan"
    else:
        return "Season Ticket Prospect"  # engaged in other ways but no ticket history

customer_summary["stage"] = customer_summary.apply(assign_stage, axis=1)

# Build per-account per-season summary
def assign_stage_season(ticket_df, retail_df):
    # Summarize tickets
    ticket_summary = (
        ticket_df.groupby(["AccountNumber", "SeasonKey"])
        .agg(
            n_purchases=("PurchaseDate", "count"),
            product_types=("ProductType", lambda x: list(x.unique()))
        )
    )
    # Summarize retail
    retail_summary = (
        retail_df.groupby("AccountNumber")["retail_spend"]
        .sum()
        .rename("retail_spend")
    )
    # Merge ticket and retail data
    summary = ticket_summary.join(retail_summary, on="AccountNumber", how="left").fillna(0)

    # Reset index to turn account_number and SeasonKey into columns
    summary = summary.reset_index()

    # Assign journey stage using your updated function
    summary["stage"] = summary.apply(assign_stage, axis=1)
    return summary

Identify Transition Probabilities

Based on the Tansition Porbabilities, the most probable movement out of a fan's prev_stage is to adjacent stages. For instance, an Engaged Fan who transitions out of that classification moves to Casual Fan or Partial Plan Holder more frequently than the other two transition possibilities.

2. Analyze Key Drivers of Engagement & Retention

Given the organization's desire for more partial-plan and season ticket members, I believe it is important to treat plan holders as being at a higher order or importance of engagement. So we should analyze what indicators tell us if a fan is going to progress or regress in their fandom.

Model Setup

Using data such as retail spending, ticket sales, and scanned ticket flags, I fit a Logisitic Regression model pipe_lr to predict if a fan will progress in their engagement (moved_up)

Interpretation

The correlation between the n_purchases and ticket_count variables means that ticket purchasing generally decreases the likelihood of a future increase in fan engagement. However, increases in ticket spending and attendance increase the likelihood of progressing to a higher tier of engagement. This makes intuitive sense because if someone is spending a lot of money on tickets, they may look for deals or plans that reduce their spending. Additionally, better attendance indicates that a fan is more engaged with Bucks basketball.
